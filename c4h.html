Civic Hacking

Code for Huntsville is Huntsville's new Code for America Brigade. We are volunteers who collaborate with local government to promote open data and build civic apps.

Get Involved

  • RSVP to our weekly Meetups
  • Collaborate with us on our first civic app, "Frontier"
  • Chat with us in Slack, our chat client

We are a growing organization and need members from all backgrounds:

  • Programmers
  • Designers
  • Government Employees
  • Lawyers
  • Thoughtful Citizens

Attend our weekly meetings on Wednesdays 6-11pm at the AL.com building in downtown Huntsville. Bring a laptop.

We will be doing some hacking and some planning. We will be discussing and storyboarding ideas, coding and generally having fun building civic applications for the Huntsville Metropolitan community.

Watch this space as we build a cool web presence, where community members can submit ideas, and watch progress on projects under development. Feel free to go to the GitHub site and download templates for civic applications (or whatever kind of application you want to build). All code on our site is open source, free to use.

In addition our WEB site will have portals and links to portals for open civic data. Open data creates a strong and informed citizenry, we didn't say that Thomas Jefferson did, so our goals is to create a place where public data is open and can be used to make great applications for a smarter city and county here in Northern Alabama.
